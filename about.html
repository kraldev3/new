On a mission to make Roblox cinematic

Shaderzone exists so any player can give Roblox a realistic visual upgrade without writing a single line of code.

The story

Shaderzone began in early 2024 inside a Discord server filled with graphics engineers and Roblox enthusiasts. The problem was clear: the Roblox renderer was operating far below the potential of modern GPUs. Community-made "RTX shaders" were either painful to install or tanked frame rates.

Our goal was simple from day one: build a post-process shader pack that is one-click to install, smartly optimized, and genuinely beautiful. Today, Shaderzone runs on over 200,000 PCs across more than 95 countries.

What we believe

Visual quality isn't a luxury — it's part of how a game is experienced. Roblox is full of incredible creativity, and we want that creativity to land on the screen the way it deserves.

  • Transparency: What our code does and which processes it touches is documented in plain language.
  • Performance: Visuals shouldn't ruin gameplay. Every preset is benchmarked on real hardware.
  • Community-driven: New presets are shaped by community feedback and shared on GitHub.
  • Safety: We don't touch Roblox's memory. Only a post-process pass is applied to the render layer.

The team

We're a small but obsessed crew of 8 people split between Istanbul, Berlin and San Francisco. Our backgrounds span graphics programming, GPU optimization, UX and community management.
